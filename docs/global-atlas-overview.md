# Global Atlas
### A Cultural Scavenger Hunt for International Student Ministry

---

## What It Is

Global Atlas is a mobile-first web app that helps ISM volunteers build genuine, memorable
connections with international students — and create a natural pathway toward spiritual
conversation.

It turns every encounter with an international student into a small act of cultural discovery:
the volunteer opens the app, taps the student's home country on a world map, and the two of
them go on a brief guided experience together — asking culturally specific questions, capturing
the student's answers, and stamping a "passport" that grows with every new connection.

The core insight: **international students feel most open when they feel genuinely known.**
Global Atlas gives volunteers the tools to actually know them.

---

## The Problem It Solves

ISM volunteers often struggle with three things:

1. **Starting the conversation.** Approaching someone from a completely different culture
   is intimidating. Most volunteers default to surface-level small talk and never go deeper.

2. **Remembering who they've met.** After a semester of campus outreach, it's hard to
   remember who was from where, what they talked about, and what matters to that person.

3. **Finding the bridge to Jesus.** Even when a good conversation happens, volunteers
   aren't always equipped to move it toward spiritual territory in a way that feels
   natural rather than forced.

Global Atlas addresses all three.

## How It Works

### 1. The Map
The app opens to an interactive world map. Every country a volunteer has connected with
lights up — bronze for one person met, silver for two, gold for three or more from
different cities. The map is both a tracking tool and a motivator: it makes the mission
visible.

### 2. The Encounter
When a volunteer meets an international student, they tap the student's home country
on the map. A guided flow begins:

- **Where are you from?** (city-level, not just country)
- **What's your name?**
- **Spiritual Roots:** *What faith tradition shaped how you grew up?*
  — Buddhist, Muslim, Hindu, Christian, Secular, Folk Religion, Other
- **The Souvenir Die:** rolled 2–3 times, landing on cultural conversation topics
  (Food, Music, Folklore, Slang, Hidden Gems, Cultural Gifts)

### 3. The Questions (AI-Powered)
This is where Global Atlas becomes something more than a contact tracker.

Each die roll generates a question that is:
- **Culturally specific** to the student's country *and* city
  (not "tell me about your food culture" — but "in Kaohsiung, street vendors during
  Ghost Month often sell specific offerings as well as food — did you grow up eating
  anything that was tied to a specific ritual or festival?")
- **Spiritually intelligent**, following the Resonate → Dissonate → Complete arc
  from Sam Chan's *Evangelism in a Skeptical World*:
  - Roll 1 affirms something beautiful in their culture
  - Roll 2 gently surfaces a longing or tension in their worldview
  - Roll 3 opens spiritual territory naturally

The questions are designed so that the student experiences genuine cultural curiosity —
and the trained volunteer recognizes the spiritual arc they're moving through.

### 4. The Passport
After the conversation, the student and volunteer take a selfie together. The entry is
saved to the volunteer's "passport" — a personal record of every person they've met,
what was shared, and what questions were asked. Every person gets their own card.

India shows three separate people — Priya from Mumbai, Arjun from Delhi, Meera from
Bangalore — each with their own story. Not just "India: gold."

## What Makes It Different

**It's built for the actual encounter, not for afterward.**
Most CRM or contact tools are used after a conversation to log notes. Global Atlas is
used *during* the conversation, with the student looking at the phone alongside the
volunteer. The student is a participant, not a subject.

**It treats culture as a bridge, not a barrier.**
Most evangelism tools ignore cultural context entirely or reduce it to "be respectful."
Global Atlas uses country, city, and religious background to generate questions that
meet students inside their actual worldview — and then ask the kind of question that
opens a door the Holy Spirit can walk through.

**It makes the mission visible and motivating.**
The map turns a semester of outreach into something you can see. A volunteer who has
met students from 23 countries has a map that shows it — not a spreadsheet.

## Current State

The app is live at **global-atlas-plum.vercel.app** and includes:

- Interactive world map with pan, zoom, and country-level mastery tracking
- Full 4-step encounter flow (city, name, spiritual roots, 2–3 die rolls)
- Person-first passport gallery — each student as an individual, not aggregated by country
- Pre-loaded demo data across 10 countries so the app demonstrates its value immediately
- First-run guided walkthrough for new users
- AI-powered culturally-specific question generation (in development)

---

## The AI Feature (In Development)

Using Google's Gemini API, each die roll generates a question specific to:
- The student's **country and city**
- Their **religious background**
- The **souvenir topic** rolled
- The **phase of the conversation** (Resonate / Dissonate / Complete)

The AI is grounded in a custom evangelism philosophy document based on Sam Chan's method,
with gospel bridges mapped to Buddhist, Muslim, Hindu, Confucian/Folk Religion, and
Secular worldviews.

The result: a volunteer who has never studied missiology still asks the kind of question
a seasoned cross-cultural worker would ask — because the app already knows the cultural
and spiritual context.

## Vision

Global Atlas is not a one-size-fits-all evangelism tool. It is a cultural intelligence
platform for ISM — one that grows more useful the more it is used, and that treats every
international student as a full person with a real story worth knowing.

The long-term vision includes:
- Follow-up conversation arc suggestions after each encounter
- Milestone rewards and progression to sustain volunteer motivation
- Exportable interaction summaries for team debrief and prayer
- Campus and team-level views for ISM leadership
